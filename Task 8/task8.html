The classical calculation treats each detector outcome as an independent Malus-law probability. The quantum prediction depends only on the relative detector angle because measurement at Detector A prepares the correlated state measured by Detector B. At θ = −30° and φ = 30°, the classical mismatch is 3/8, while the quantum mismatch is 3/4.
