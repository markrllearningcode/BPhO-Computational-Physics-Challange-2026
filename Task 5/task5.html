Bohr energy levels

En = −13.6057 Z²/n² eV
Eγ = En − Em
Eγ = 13.6057 Z²(1/m² − 1/n²)
An emitted photon is produced when an electron moves from a higher initial level n to a lower final level m. The energy difference fixes the photon frequency and wavelength.

Spectrum interpretation

Eγ = hf = hc/λ
Lyman transitions end at m = 1, Balmer at m = 2, Paschen at m = 3, Brackett at m = 4 and Pfund at m = 5. For hydrogen, the Balmer series contains the strongest familiar visible lines.
Constants: h = 6.62607015×10⁻³⁴ J s, c = 2.99792458×10⁸ m s⁻¹, e = 1.602176634×10⁻¹⁹ C, hydrogen ground-state magnitude = 13.605693 eV.
