Physics model

Photon energy: E = hf = hc/λ
Einstein equation: Kmax = hf − φ
Stopping potential: eVs = Kmax
Threshold: f0 = φ/h, λ0 = hc/φ
Emission occurs only when one photon has enough energy to overcome the work function. Intensity changes the photon rate and saturation current, but not the maximum kinetic energy.

Current model

Electron energies are spread from zero to Kmax. Positive voltage collects all emitted electrons; negative voltage removes lower-energy electrons until the current becomes zero at −Vs. This is a simplified teaching model. On a Vs-against-f graph, the gradient is h/e.
Constants: h = 6.62607015×10⁻³⁴ J s, e = 1.602176634×10⁻¹⁹ C, c = 2.99792458×10⁸ m s⁻¹, mₑ = 9.1093837015×10⁻³¹ kg.
